Student D

"I would recommend College of Management, for both those on the business side, and the Information Technology side. This college, over the Computer Science college, allows IT students to focus more on the general business aspect of IT that occur in every day life; from project management to analyzing data. This college also doesn't require a lot of coding classes compared to the CS college, if there are any students worried about that.

I chose this college as I've had years of experience in IT before hand, doing many summer job programs at UMass Boston itself, and after knowing that coding and the courses they give in the CS college wasn't meant for me, I saw courses that interest me more in the College of Management; such as Ethics in IT, Computer Forensics, and Cyber Crime to list a few. While these can be taken in the CS college as well, they aren't recommend as much.

I'd say that the College of Management IT not requiring students to take the writing proficiency exam is definitely one of my favorite parts, where they replace it with Business Communications course; which helps me in real life situations and how I should deal with them instead of taking a standardized exam (which I am not a fan of) A small tidbit of experience in this college would be that you will take more business related courses, like economics or project management, but if that's where you see yourself wanting to go with a hint of IT work, then the College of Management is the right one to pick."
